# SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure Rules Compliance Checklist

A detailed, actionable checklist for public companies subject to the SEC's cybersecurity disclosure rules (Release No. 33-11216). Organized by compliance area with specific action items for Form 8-K Item 1.05 incident disclosure and Form 10-K Item 1C annual governance reporting.

Last Reviewed: March 2026

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Determine your filer status (large accelerated, accelerated, non-accelerated, smaller reporting company, foreign private issuer).
2. Work through each section, marking items as complete, in progress, or not started.
3. For incomplete items, assign an owner and target completion date.
4. Conduct tabletop exercises to validate your incident response and disclosure processes.
5. Review and update this checklist at least annually, and after any material cybersecurity incident.

---

## 1. Governance Structure

### Board Oversight
- [ ] Designate board committee responsible for cybersecurity risk oversight (audit committee, risk committee, or dedicated cyber committee)
- [ ] Ensure committee charter explicitly includes cybersecurity oversight responsibilities
- [ ] Schedule regular cybersecurity briefings to the board (at minimum, quarterly)
- [ ] Define criteria for ad hoc board notification of cybersecurity matters
- [ ] Document how the board receives information about cybersecurity risks
- [ ] Assess board members' cybersecurity knowledge and experience
- [ ] Consider adding cybersecurity expertise to board composition criteria
- [ ] Establish board access to external cybersecurity advisors

### Management Structure
- [ ] Designate a Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) or equivalent role
- [ ] Document CISO's reporting structure (direct report to CEO, CIO, or board committee)
- [ ] Define CISO's qualifications, experience, and relevant certifications
- [ ] Establish CISO's authority to escalate issues directly to the board
- [ ] Document management's role in cybersecurity risk assessment and management
- [ ] Define cross-functional cybersecurity governance team (CISO, General Counsel, CFO, CIO, CHRO)
- [ ] Establish regular management-level cybersecurity risk review meetings
- [ ] Document how management monitors prevention, detection, mitigation, and remediation of incidents

### Disclosure Controls
- [ ] Update disclosure controls and procedures (DC&P) to include cybersecurity incidents
- [ ] Integrate cybersecurity incident assessment into the DC&P framework
- [ ] Define escalation path from incident detection to disclosure decision
- [ ] Identify officers responsible for certifying cybersecurity disclosures (CEO and CFO per SOX)
- [ ] Establish communication protocols between CISO, General Counsel, CFO, and external counsel
- [ ] Document the role of the disclosure committee in cybersecurity matters

## 2. Risk Management and Strategy

### Risk Assessment Process
- [ ] Implement a cybersecurity risk management framework (NIST CSF, NIST SP 800-53, ISO 27001, or equivalent)
- [ ] Integrate cybersecurity risk assessment into enterprise risk management (ERM) program
- [ ] Conduct annual comprehensive cybersecurity risk assessments
- [ ] Identify and document material cybersecurity risks
- [ ] Assess cybersecurity risks from third-party service providers
- [ ] Evaluate cybersecurity risks associated with company products and services
- [ ] Assess cybersecurity risks related to intellectual property and trade secrets
- [ ] Consider AI-specific cybersecurity risks (adversarial AI, deepfakes, AI-powered attacks)
- [ ] Document risk acceptance decisions and rationale

### Third-Party Risk Management
- [ ] Maintain inventory of third-party service providers with access to company systems or data
- [ ] Implement vendor cybersecurity assessment process (questionnaires, audits, certifications)
- [ ] Require cybersecurity contractual provisions in vendor agreements
- [ ] Monitor ongoing vendor cybersecurity posture
- [ ] Define incident notification requirements in vendor contracts
- [ ] Assess concentration risk from critical single-vendor dependencies
- [ ] Include supply chain cybersecurity in risk assessments

### Framework Alignment
- [ ] Document which cybersecurity framework(s) the company uses
- [ ] Map existing controls to chosen framework
- [ ] Identify and document control gaps
- [ ] Maintain remediation roadmap for identified gaps
- [ ] Conduct periodic framework maturity assessments
- [ ] Document how framework implementation informs business strategy and capital allocation

## 3. Incident Response and Disclosure Process

### Incident Response Plan
- [ ] Maintain documented incident response plan aligned with SEC disclosure timelines
- [ ] Define incident severity levels and corresponding response procedures
- [ ] Establish 24/7 incident detection and initial response capability
- [ ] Define roles and responsibilities for incident response team
- [ ] Include legal counsel (internal and external) in incident response procedures
- [ ] Include public/investor relations in incident response procedures
- [ ] Include forensic investigation capabilities (internal or contracted)
- [ ] Define evidence preservation procedures
- [ ] Establish communication protocols (internal, law enforcement, regulators, customers)
- [ ] Define criteria for engaging external incident response firms

### Materiality Determination Process
- [ ] Develop documented materiality determination framework
- [ ] Define quantitative materiality thresholds (dollar amounts, percentage of revenue, EPS impact)
- [ ] Define qualitative materiality factors (reputation, competitive impact, regulatory consequences)
- [ ] Establish cross-functional materiality assessment team (legal, finance, IT, business leaders)
- [ ] Define aggregation criteria for related incidents
- [ ] Document the process for making materiality determinations "without unreasonable delay"
- [ ] Create materiality determination templates and worksheets
- [ ] Establish legal privilege protocols for materiality discussions
- [ ] Define process for reassessing materiality as new information becomes available
- [ ] Document all materiality determinations (positive and negative) with supporting rationale

### Form 8-K Item 1.05 Filing Process
- [ ] Create pre-drafted Form 8-K Item 1.05 disclosure template
- [ ] Define four-business-day filing workflow with assigned responsibilities
- [ ] Identify individuals authorized to approve Form 8-K filing
- [ ] Establish process for SEC filing mechanics (EDGAR access, filing codes)
- [ ] Define process for determining whether national security delay is warranted
- [ ] Establish FBI contact for national security delay requests
- [ ] Define process for filing Form 8-K/A amendments when material updates occur
- [ ] Document process for avoiding premature or excessive technical disclosures
- [ ] Coordinate with investor relations on concurrent investor communications
- [ ] Coordinate with media relations on concurrent public communications

### Testing and Exercises
- [ ] Conduct tabletop exercises at least annually simulating material incident disclosure
- [ ] Include board members in at least one annual tabletop exercise
- [ ] Test the end-to-end process from detection through materiality determination to 8-K filing
- [ ] Test communication protocols between all stakeholders
- [ ] Test after-hours and weekend notification procedures
- [ ] Document lessons learned and update procedures based on exercise findings
- [ ] Simulate scenarios involving third-party vendor incidents
- [ ] Simulate aggregation scenarios (multiple related incidents)

## 4. Annual Form 10-K Item 1C Disclosure

### Risk Management and Strategy Narrative (Item 106(b))
- [ ] Describe cybersecurity risk assessment processes
- [ ] Explain how cybersecurity is integrated into overall risk management
- [ ] Describe use of third-party assessors, consultants, or auditors
- [ ] Describe processes for overseeing third-party cybersecurity risks
- [ ] Describe how previous incidents have informed risk management
- [ ] Explain how cybersecurity risk management informs business strategy
- [ ] Identify any cybersecurity frameworks used (NIST CSF, ISO 27001, etc.)
- [ ] Avoid boilerplate language; provide specific, substantive disclosures
- [ ] Include information about cybersecurity insurance coverage (if material)
- [ ] Describe key cybersecurity investments and initiatives

### Governance Narrative (Item 106(c))
- [ ] Describe board's role in overseeing cybersecurity risks
- [ ] Identify specific board committee(s) responsible for cybersecurity
- [ ] Describe how the board is informed about cybersecurity risks (frequency, format)
- [ ] Describe management's role in assessing and managing cybersecurity risks
- [ ] Describe relevant expertise of management responsible for cybersecurity
- [ ] Describe how management monitors incident prevention, detection, mitigation, remediation
- [ ] Describe management's reporting to board on cybersecurity matters
- [ ] Avoid disclosing specific vulnerabilities or defense details

### Drafting and Review
- [ ] Engage external cybersecurity counsel in 10-K drafting
- [ ] Have CISO review technical accuracy of cybersecurity disclosures
- [ ] Have General Counsel review for legal sufficiency and risk
- [ ] Have CFO review for consistency with financial disclosures
- [ ] Compare disclosures against peer companies for industry consistency
- [ ] Ensure disclosures are consistent with other public statements about cybersecurity
- [ ] Validate that disclosures accurately reflect current state (not aspirational)
- [ ] Obtain CEO and CFO certification per SOX Section 302

## 5. Ongoing Compliance Maintenance

### Documentation and Recordkeeping
- [ ] Maintain records of all cybersecurity incidents (material and non-material)
- [ ] Document all materiality determinations with supporting analysis
- [ ] Retain board and committee meeting minutes reflecting cybersecurity discussions
- [ ] Maintain records of cybersecurity training provided to board and management
- [ ] Document all changes to cybersecurity risk management processes
- [ ] Retain incident response exercise reports and lessons learned
- [ ] Maintain vendor cybersecurity assessment records
- [ ] Archive all SEC filings related to cybersecurity (8-Ks, 10-Ks, amendments)

### Monitoring and Updates
- [ ] Monitor SEC enforcement actions and guidance related to cybersecurity disclosure
- [ ] Monitor SEC staff comments on cybersecurity disclosures in other companies' filings
- [ ] Update materiality thresholds annually based on company financial metrics
- [ ] Review and update incident response plan at least annually
- [ ] Update disclosure templates to reflect regulatory changes
- [ ] Assess impact of new cyber threats on materiality criteria
- [ ] Review D&O insurance coverage for cybersecurity disclosure liability
- [ ] Monitor shareholder litigation related to cybersecurity disclosures

### Training
- [ ] Train incident response team on SEC disclosure requirements
- [ ] Train board members on their cybersecurity oversight responsibilities
- [ ] Train management on materiality determination framework
- [ ] Train legal team on intersection of cybersecurity law and securities law
- [ ] Train investor relations on cybersecurity incident communication
- [ ] Conduct annual refresher training for all involved personnel
- [ ] Brief external auditors on cybersecurity disclosure processes

## 6. Foreign Private Issuer Requirements

If applicable (foreign private issuers only):
- [ ] Report material cybersecurity incidents on Form 6-K
- [ ] Provide annual cybersecurity governance disclosures on Form 20-F
- [ ] Determine whether home country cybersecurity disclosure requirements overlap
- [ ] Coordinate SEC disclosure with home country regulatory requirements
- [ ] Address any conflicts between SEC and home country disclosure timelines

## 7. Integration with Other Compliance Frameworks

### NIST Alignment
- [ ] Map NIST CSF 2.0 functions (Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover) to SEC disclosure requirements
- [ ] Ensure NIST SP 800-53 incident response controls (IR family) support SEC timelines
- [ ] Leverage NIST SP 800-61 incident handling guidance for SEC-aligned IR procedures
- [ ] Use NIST SP 800-30 risk assessment methodology to inform materiality determinations

### SOX Integration
- [ ] Integrate cybersecurity incident assessment into SOX 302 certification process
- [ ] Include cybersecurity in SOX 404 internal control testing
- [ ] Coordinate cybersecurity disclosures with financial reporting controls
- [ ] Ensure IT general controls (ITGCs) support cybersecurity disclosure reliability

### Industry-Specific Requirements
- [ ] Coordinate SEC disclosure with HIPAA breach notification (if healthcare)
- [ ] Coordinate SEC disclosure with PCI DSS incident requirements (if payment processing)
- [ ] Coordinate SEC disclosure with GLBA/FTC Safeguards Rule (if financial services)
- [ ] Coordinate SEC disclosure with state breach notification laws
- [ ] Coordinate SEC disclosure with GDPR breach notification (if EU data involved)
- [ ] Coordinate SEC disclosure with CMMC/DFARS incident reporting (if defense contractor)
